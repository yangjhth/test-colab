## ðŸŒ³ Tree Planting

### Current Status
âœ… **Trees and sites are prepared** - Ready to go!  
ðŸŽ¯ **Focus needed**: Initial energy burst for planting, then maintenance routine

### Key Success Factors
1. **Energy Management**
   - Wait for a day when you feel energized
   - Don't force it when exhausted
   - Consider planting as morning activity (cooler, more energy)
   - Break into multiple sessions if many trees

2. **Planting Best Practices**
   - Check soil moisture before planting
   - Dig holes 2-3x width of root ball
   - Water immediately after planting
   - Mulch around base (but not touching trunk)
   - Stake if needed for stability
   - Take "before" photos

3. **Maintenance Schedule**
   **First Month** (Critical):
   - Water 2-3x per week (deep watering)
   - Check for pests/disease
   - Adjust stakes if needed
   
   **Months 2-12**:
   - Water weekly (adjust for rain)
   - Weed around base
   - Monitor growth
   - Photo documentation monthly
   
   **Year 2+**:
   - Water during dry spells
   - Prune as needed
   - Annual health check
   - Celebrate growth!

### Weekly Actions
- **Pre-planting**: Monitor energy levels, check weather
- **Planting phase**: Schedule the day, gather tools, execute
- **Maintenance phase**: Set weekly reminder, create routine

### Motivation Boosters
- Think about the legacy you're creating
- Imagine the trees in 10, 20, 50 years
- Consider the wildlife that will benefit
- Meditate while watering/caring for trees

### Current Status

### The 4-Phase Journey

#### Phase 1: Local Learning (Current Phase)
**Goal**: Learn basics without equipment investment

**Actions**:
1. **Research Local Resources** (This week!)
   - Search for makerspaces in your area
   - Check public libraries (many have 3D printers now)
   - Look for community colleges offering workshops
   - Join local maker Facebook groups

2. **Online Education** (Parallel track)
   - Free tutorials: YouTube, Thingiverse tutorials
   - Beginner courses: Tinkercad (browser-based, free)
   - Learn terminology and basics
   - Understand file types (STL, OBJ, etc.)

3. **First Designs**
   - Start with Tinkercad (easiest)
   - Create simple geometric shapes
   - Modify existing designs from Thingiverse
   - Design something personally meaningful

4. **Test Prints**
   - Book time at local makerspace/library
   - Print your first designs
   - Learn about materials (PLA is easiest)
   - Understand common issues

**Success Metrics**:
- [ ] Found local printing resource
- [ ] Completed 3 online tutorials
- [ ] Created 3 original designs
- [ ] Successfully printed at local facility

#### Phase 2: Equipment Acquisition
**When to start**: After 5-10 successful prints at local facility

**Actions**:
1. **Research** (2-4 weeks)
   - Compare printer models (Ender 3, Prusa, etc.)
   - Read reviews and join Reddit communities
   - Set realistic budget ($200-$500 for beginners)
   - Understand space requirements

2. **Preparation**
   - Designate workspace at home
   - Ensure ventilation
   - Plan for storage of materials
   - Save funds if needed

3. **Purchase & Setup**
   - Buy printer, filament, tools
   - Careful assembly/setup
   - Calibration (patience required!)
   - First home prints

#### Phase 3: Technique Mastery
**Focus**: Build consistent skill and portfolio

**Regular Practice**:
- Weekly printing sessions (minimum)
- Experiment with different:
  - Materials (PLA, PETG, TPU)
  - Layer heights
  - Infill patterns
  - Support structures

**Design Skills**:
- Progress from Tinkercad to Fusion 360 or Blender
- Learn more advanced techniques
- Create increasingly complex designs
- Develop personal style

**Documentation**:
- Photo each creation
- Note what works and what doesn't
- Build Instagram or portfolio
- Share in maker communities

## ðŸŽ¯ Integration Strategy

### Don't Do Everything at Once!
These are called "pillars" for a reason - they support your life over time. You don't need to make progress on all three every day.

### Suggested Weekly Pattern

**Option 1: Rotating Focus**
- Week 1-2: Focus primarily on one pillar
- Week 3-4: Rotate to different pillar
- Maintain others with minimal actions

**Option 2: Project-Based**
- When in crabbing season: prioritize fishing trips
- When weather/energy good: focus on tree planting
- During winter/evenings: work on 3D printing

**Option 3: Balanced Approach**
- Monday: 3D printing tutorial (30 min)
- Wednesday: Tree maintenance check (20 min)
- Saturday: Family fishing trip coordination (1 hour)

### Daily Plan Integration
Each day in your daily plan, ask:
- "Which pillar needs attention this week?"
- "Which pillar am I excited about today?"
- "Which pillar has a time-sensitive action?"

Choose ONE to focus on, make progress, then move on.
